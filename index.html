A decentralized exchange, or DEX for short, is a peer-to-peer marketplace where crypto traders make transactions directly between one another. These transactions are not officiated by banks, brokers, or any other intermediary. A trader can trade one crypto currency token for another directly on the blockchain. Tokens traders go to your virtual wallet.

In 2021, over $200 billion USD in transactions flowed through decentralized exchanges. It’s safe to say, they are the next big thing in finance. There are over two million DeFi traders, and it’s growing by the millions each quarter.

As a liquidity provider, you add crypto to the liquidity pool and reap rewards. The fee you earn is proportional to the amount of liquidity they have supplied, usually set at 3% but could be lower or higher.
